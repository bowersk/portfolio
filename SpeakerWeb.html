Hanging speaker holders for ME 203

(under construction)This is my final project for ME218A: mechatronic design part A. I created a quadruple slider-crank mechanism, powered by a continuous rotation servo, which actuates ball bearings into four electromagnet’s zones of magnetic reach. A numpad (which I bought from amazon and ripped out the usb circuitry so I could use the numpad simply as an array of switches) turns each electromagnet on or off, and a potentiometer knob controls the direction and speed of the slider crank mechanism. When you get a ball bearing stuck to a magnet, an IR LED sensor tells the TIVA microcontroller (controlling the entire system) to turn on the indication LED corresponding to the magnet number.

My next steps were to make a game out of this whole thing, e.g. the microcontroller would control the slider-crank irradically and the potentiometer would dampen the chaotic motor drive. You would have to stick all 4 ball bearings to the magnets within 30 seconds of touching the machine. But, I unfortunately ran out of time. This was a difficult project. 12/5/2014
